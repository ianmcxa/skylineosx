Additional Resources

There are a lot of great hackintosh sites out there. Here's a list of the ones I find particularly helpful


Guides Communities Blogs Other Resources

Guides

Site

Description

OSX Latitude

The only hackintosh forum dedicated to installing on laptops. Osxlatitude contains a forum as well as lists and guides for compatible laptops and prebuilt desktops. 

The Clover Wiki

This is the documentation for the Clover bootloader. It has lots of detailed information; however, it is hard to understand and not entirely complete. 

Communities

/r/hackintosh

The perfect place to ask for help. /r/Hackintosh is a subreddit within the reddit community. They are generally very helpful. /r/Hackintosh will also review your build if you ask nicely. My one qualm with the community is the lack of answers to advanced questions. 

Project OSX

Another advanced forum. There is a lot of discussion about Clover and it’s development. 

Insanelymac

An excellent forum site. They have tons of guides and info, especially on advanced topics. There is a chat, which can be a great source of info depending on who’s online. However, insanelymac is not noob friendly and requires you to take a quiz (albeit an easy one) on computing before you can post. 

Blogs

MacBreaker

Macbreaker creates install guides, as well as tips and tricks for hackintoshes. He supports all install methods.

Rampage Dev

RampageDev’s blog has DSDT’s and custom install guides for x79 motherboards. It has recently expanded quite a bit with loads of new guides and articles. While x79 is his focus, many of his guides apply to all hackintoshes.

Misc

HackintoshOSX

Probably the most extensive hackintosh download site. Most kexts and patches can be found here. The forum is much like that of insanelymac.
